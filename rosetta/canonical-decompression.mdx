Two different models must reconstruct the same document from the same packets.

## Three Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 1: Packet to Claim">
    Each packet becomes one natural language assertion. The operation determines the template:

    - **OBS**: "[agent] observes [subject] is [value] with [CC]% confidence."
    - **INF**: "[agent] concludes [conclusion] based on [evidence]."
    - **CON**: "[agent] disagrees with [target] because [counter-evidence]."
    - **MRG**: "[agent] synthesizes [sources]: [merged claim]."
    - **YLD**: "[agent] changed from [old] to [new] because [cause]."
    - **PRD**: "[agent] predicts [outcome] with [CC]% confidence within [timeframe]."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 2: Claims to Sections">
    Group claims by SUBJ tag. Order by CC descending within each group. Each group becomes a section with a header derived from the common subject.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 3: Sections to Document">
    Connect sections with transitional prose. Restore rhetorical flow. Introduce with executive summary (highest-CC PRD packets). Conclude with synthesis (MRG packets). Preserve all named entities, numbers, and causal chains from original packets.
  </Step>
</Steps>
